SAMBA

SAMBA is an IEQ monitoring system developed by Tom Parkinson, a researcher at the University of California (Berkeley) and the deputy director of the IEQ Lab at the University of Sydney. His small and inconspicuous device offers continuous monitoring of a range of IEQ factors across the day, and is relatively cheap.

SAMBA works by forming a mesh network that sends measurements to a ‘bridge’ device, that then uploads this data to a cloud. This is finally outputted as an easy to operate dashboard for people at all levels of management to follow up on.
